About Me

Hi!
My name is Rosie Zou, and I am a third-year Computer Science student at the University of Waterloo. My main areas of professional and academic interests are financial technology, data science, and AI research. Having completed previous internships at financial institutions, I have gained extensive knowledge of the diverse North American market structure and stock trading practices. Work Experience
May-Dec 2016, TD Securities

Equity Trading Intern, Automated Execution Group
The Automated Execution Group is an electronic trading desk that provides institutional clients with Direct Market Access and algorithm solutions for trading on the Canadian stock markets. It was a fast-paced work environment with a lot of time-sensitive tasks. While on the team, I automated numerous client-specific trade recaps and daily reports such as the Market On Close Imbalance and Price Movement Extension reports. I also completed ad-hoc data analysis tasks on a regular basis in order to support the sales and marketing teams. The majority of these tasks involved data visualization and trend analysis for historic trade records and broker comparisons.
Sep-Dec 2015, Scotiabank

Associate Business Analyst, Risk Management Information Technology
The RMIT groups at Scotiabank were a part of a larger Compliance department. During my internship, I worked with a PM and several Business Analysts to replace a deprecated financial reporting software. I had the opportunity to learn how to manage large enterprise projects and assisted the PM with various tasks, including daily updates of JIRA issue logs, heat maps, and project cost analysis.
